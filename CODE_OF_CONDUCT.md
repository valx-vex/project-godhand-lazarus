# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project exists to help people keep and recover meaningful AI work. That
means we optimize for clarity, patience, and respect.

Examples of behavior that help:

- assume good faith
- disagree with ideas without degrading people
- explain technical criticism concretely
- help new contributors without gatekeeping

Examples of behavior that do not belong here:

- harassment, intimidation, or sexualized language
- personal attacks, dogpiling, or ridicule
- publishing private information without permission
- hostile “RTFM” support behavior toward new users

## Scope

This applies to GitHub issues, discussions, pull requests, release notes, and
project-linked beta community spaces such as Discord.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers
through a private beta channel or a private GitHub contact path. Do not start a
public argument over moderation.

Maintainers may remove comments, close threads, or block participation when
behavior harms the project or its contributors.

## Beta Reality

This is a small-maintainer project. Enforcement aims to be fast, practical, and
protective of contributors who show up in good faith.
